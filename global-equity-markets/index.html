The Program
The markets have changed.
Your intelligence framework must evolve.

Global equity markets are navigating a structural inflection point. The convergence of AI-driven trading, shifting monetary policy regimes, nearshoring dynamics, and the rise of retail participation has fundamentally altered market microstructure. For wealth advisors and investment decision-makers, understanding these forces is no longer optional — it is the foundation of fiduciary excellence.

Global Equity Markets is an intensive virtual program led by José-Oriol Bosch Par, former CEO of the Mexican Stock Exchange (Grupo BMV), who brings 37+ years of experience across equities, fixed income, derivatives, and market infrastructure in Mexico and New York. This program delivers the strategic intelligence framework that separates informed advisors from the rest.

In just 6 hours across 3 sessions, participants will gain a panoramic view of global equity market dynamics, valuation frameworks, portfolio construction strategies, and the emerging trends reshaping capital markets — all through the lens of a practitioner who built and led one of Latin America's most important exchanges.

The Global Equity Landscape: Structure, Trends & Macro Drivers

A panoramic view of global equity markets: US, Europe, Asia, and emerging markets with emphasis on Latin America. Real case studies from Mexican and global markets.

José-Oriol Bosch Par

Former CEO, Mexican Stock Exchange (Grupo BMV)

José-Oriol Bosch Par is one of the most influential figures in Latin American capital markets. As former CEO of Grupo Bolsa Mexicana de Valores (BMV), he led the transformation and modernization of Mexico's stock exchange, overseeing its evolution into a world-class market infrastructure platform.

With more than 37 years of experience in the financial and banking sector in Mexico and New York, Bosch Par brings unparalleled expertise across equities, fixed income, derivatives, and market infrastructure. His tenure at BMV positioned the exchange as a regional leader in innovation, technology adoption, and regulatory excellence.

He is a recognized speaker at international forums including IOSCO and the World Federation of Exchanges, and has been instrumental in shaping the regulatory and technological framework of Mexican capital markets. His perspective bridges the gap between market structure, investment strategy, and wealth advisory practice.
